Our Story

The Inner Palace of Bengal

Antarmahal was born from a conviction — that the finest craft traditions of Bengal deserve a home in the modern world. We are not a brand. We are a bridge.

"Every thread carries a memory, every chisel stroke a prayer."
— Rukmini Devi, Master Kantha Artisan
Who We Are

Keepers of a Living Heritage

Antarmahal — the inner chamber — is a word that evokes the private sanctum of a Bengali household. A space where stories are told in whispers, where a grandmother's hands move across silk with the certainty of a lifetime, where a sculptor's devotion becomes visible in clay.

We were founded in 2019 by a collective of cultural researchers, textile scholars, and design thinkers who saw what was being lost. As fast fashion flooded markets and craft clusters collapsed, generations of irreplaceable knowledge began to disappear quietly.

We work directly with artisan families across West Bengal — in Birbhum, Murshidabad, Bankura, and Bishnupur — creating relationships that are fair, long-term, and rooted in mutual respect.

Every piece we sell carries a story. Every purchase supports a family. Every stitch and sculpture is a small act of preservation in a world moving too fast to look back.

Our Principles

What We Stand For

01
Radical Transparency
We name our artisans. We share their stories. We show you exactly where your money goes and what it means to the people who made your piece.
02
Fair & Direct Trade
No middlemen. No exploitation. Our artisans receive 60–70% of the retail price — a radical departure from the industry norm of 8–12%.
03
Living Craft
We invest in apprenticeship programmes, documentation, and craft education to ensure these traditions thrive for the next generation — not just survive.
